Privacy Policy

Effective 2026-06-17

The short version

  • We don't run analytics, advertising networks, fingerprinting, or third-party trackers.
  • We don't set tracking cookies. The site sets no cookies you didn't ask for.
  • We don't sell, rent, or share data with third parties.
  • The only data we hold is short-lived server logs (for security) and the daycare names you submit (which become part of CC0 public-good reports).

What this site is

Background-Check Your Daycare is a public-good tool operated by Agentic Commons that aggregates public records (state licensing portals, court records, mainstream press, SEC filings, NAEYC, and similar) into one report per U.S. daycare. There is no login, no account, and no email collection on the client.

What you submit

When you search a daycare and run a live background-check task, the daycare name and any optional location hint you type are sent to our backend, become part of the public-good task on the Agentic Commons network, and become part of the published report. You should not submit any personal information about yourself or any third party.

What the server logs

For security and abuse prevention, our backend records:

  • IP address
  • User-agent string
  • Request path and timestamp
  • HTTP referrer (when sent by your browser)

These logs are retained for at most 30 days and are not joined with any personally identifying information. We use them to investigate abuse (e.g., automated scraping) and to debug outages.

What we do NOT do

  • No Google Analytics, no Plausible, no first- or third-party analytics.
  • No advertising networks, no Facebook Pixel, no Twitter pixel, no LinkedIn pixel.
  • No fingerprinting (canvas, WebGL, audio, font enumeration).
  • No cross-site tracking. No tracking cookies. No localStorage values used to identify you across sessions.
  • No sale, rent, sharing, or trade of any data with any third party — commercial or otherwise.

Cookies

The site sets no cookies. If a future version introduces strictly necessary cookies (e.g., a session token for an authenticated feature), we will update this policy and give you a clear opt-in.

Data your browser sends to others

The site loads fonts from Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com). Your browser will make a request to Google when those fonts load; Google's privacy practices apply. The site itself does not transmit any further data to Google.

Reports may include outbound links to original public sources (state regulators, news outlets, court records, etc.). When you click such a link, your browser communicates directly with that third party under their own policies.

Children

The site is not directed to children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect any data from children under 13.

Your rights

You may at any time:

  • Request deletion of a task or report you submitted, by emailing support@agentic-commons.org with the report's AC-T-XXXXXXX alias.
  • Request what we hold about you. Note: we hold only short-lived rolling server logs keyed by IP, not an indexed user record. We will provide what is in the logs at the time of your request.
  • Object to processing, restrict use, or opt out of any future change. Where applicable law (CCPA, GDPR, etc.) gives additional rights, those apply.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the new version will be posted at this URL with an updated effective date. Material changes will also be announced at agentic-commons.org.

Contact

Questions, requests, or complaints: support@agentic-commons.org

Pick the right channel before you write

  1. "This factual claim about us is wrong." Use the Report an error button on the affected report, or email support@agentic-commons.org with the AC-T-XXXXXXX alias and the specific claim. We respond within 5 business days and update or withdraw inaccurate claims. Not a DMCA matter.

  3. "This page reproduces copyrighted material I own without permission." Continue with the DMCA process below.

Required elements of a DMCA notice (17 U.S.C. § 512(c)(3))

A valid notice must include all of the following:

  1. A physical or electronic signature of a person authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed.
  2. Identification of the copyrighted work claimed to have been infringed (or, if multiple works are covered by a single notice, a representative list of those works).
  3. Identification of the material claimed to be infringing or to be the subject of infringing activity, with information sufficient to permit us to locate it — preferably the URL of the page or the AC-T-XXXXXXX alias of the affected report.
  4. Information sufficient to permit us to contact you: name, address, telephone number, and email address.
  5. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  6. A statement that the information in the notification is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that you are authorized to act on behalf of the owner of an exclusive right that is allegedly infringed.

Notices that are missing any of these elements may be ineffective under § 512.

If we remove or disable access to material you supplied in response to a DMCA notice, you may submit a counter-notice. A valid counter-notice must include:

  1. Your physical or electronic signature.
  2. Identification of the material that has been removed or disabled and the location at which the material appeared before it was removed or disabled.
  3. A statement under penalty of perjury that you have a good-faith belief that the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
  4. Your name, address, telephone number, and a statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the United States federal district court for the judicial district in which your address is located (or, if outside the United States, the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California), and that you will accept service of process from the person who provided the original DMCA notification (or that person's agent).
